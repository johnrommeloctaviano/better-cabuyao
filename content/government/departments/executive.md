# Cabuyao City Executive Department

The Executive Department is the action arm of the Cabuyao City Government. It is responsible for enforcing all city laws and ordinances, implementing programs and projects, managing all city departments and employees, and delivering public services to residents.

**Legal basis:** Republic Act No. 10163 (Charter of Cabuyao City) and the Local Government Code of 1991.

---

# The Mayor and Vice Mayor

The **City Mayor** is the Chief Executive of Cabuyao City, elected by residents during the **Philippine National and Local Elections** held every three years.

The Mayor's duties include:

- Enforcing all city laws, ordinances, and resolutions
- Leading all city departments and appointing department heads
- Approving city spending and signing contracts
- Overseeing the implementation of development programs
- Representing the city in official functions

# How Executive Power Flows

```
Citizens → Elect Mayor
Mayor → Appoints Department Heads
Departments → Deliver City Services
Barangays → Coordinate Locally (under Mayor's supervision)
```

The Mayor can **veto** ordinances passed by the Sangguniang Panlungsod. The Council can **override** a veto by a two-thirds vote. The executive branch then implements whatever the legislative branch enacts.

# Invite the Mayor to Your Event

Residents, organizations, and institutions may invite the Mayor to attend official community activities such as community celebrations, school programs, civic ceremonies, and government events.

To request the Mayor's attendance, submit a formal invitation letter to the **Office of the Mayor**, 2nd Floor, Cabuyao City Hall, at least two weeks before the event. Include:

- Name of the event and organizer
- Date, time, and venue
- Expected number of attendees
- Purpose of the invitation
