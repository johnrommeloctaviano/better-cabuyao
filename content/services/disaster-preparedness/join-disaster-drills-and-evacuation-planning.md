# Cabuyao City: Disaster Drills and Community Preparedness

The **Cabuyao City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)** and barangay DRRM committees conduct regular drills, training, and preparedness programs open to all residents.

---

## Barangay DRRM Committees

Every barangay in Cabuyao has its own **DRRM Committee**, chaired by the Barangay Captain, responsible for:

- Maintaining the barangay's evacuation lists
- Conducting annual and seasonal evacuation drills
- Identifying local hazard zones (flood-prone streets, fire risks)
- Managing the local evacuation center
- Coordinating with the City DRRMO during emergencies

**Your first contact for disaster preparedness:** your Barangay Captain and Barangay Hall.

---

## Drills and Training Programs

### Community Evacuation Drills

- Conducted at barangay level (coordinated with CDRRMO)
- Practice routes to designated evacuation centers
- Schools participate through **DepEd** earthquake and fire drills

### CDRRMO Training Programs

| Program                                 | What It Covers                           |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Basic Life Support (BLS)                | CPR, first aid, basic emergency response |
| Water Rescue Training                   | Flood rescue techniques for volunteers   |
| Earthquake Drill (Drop, Cover, Hold On) | Earthquake immediate response            |
| Incident Command System (ICS)           | For DRRM staff and barangay officials    |

### Fire Prevention Month Activities

The **Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)** and CDRRMO conduct fire prevention seminars and emergency response readiness campaigns annually (active in 2026).

---

## How to Join Drills or Request Training

1. **Monitor your Barangay Facebook page and Barangay Hall** for scheduled drills.
2. **Contact the CDRRMO** to inquire about upcoming community training programs open to residents.
3. **Schools and organizations** can coordinate directly with the CDRRMO for group drills and emergency preparedness sessions.

---

## Prepare Your Go-Bag Now

Don't wait for a drill to be prepared. Assemble a go-bag with:

- Water (enough for 2–3 days)
- Canned goods and ready-to-eat food
- Flashlight and extra batteries
- Power bank (fully charged)
- Change of clothes
- Important IDs and documents in a waterproof pouch
- Basic medicines (maintenance medications, first aid kit)

**Know your evacuation center:** Go to your Barangay Hall and ask which school or covered court serves as your primary evacuation point.
