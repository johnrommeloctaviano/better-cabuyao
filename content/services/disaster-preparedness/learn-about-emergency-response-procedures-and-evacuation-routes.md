# Cabuyao City: Emergency Response and Evacuation Procedures

This guide covers what to do before, during, and after a disaster in Cabuyao City — including flood procedures, evacuation routes, and how to request rescue.

---

## Cabuyao's Main Hazards (Know Your Risk)

| Hazard             | Risk Level       | Most Affected Areas                                                                                      |
| ------------------ | ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Flooding           | **Highest risk** | Marinig (all puroks), lakeshore zones, Barangay Diez, Nueve, Siete, low-lying interior roads near canals |
| Earthquakes        | High (secondary) | City-wide                                                                                                |
| Typhoons           | Seasonal         | City-wide                                                                                                |
| Urban fires        | Moderate         | Dense residential areas                                                                                  |
| Industrial hazards | Moderate         | Pulo, Mamatid industrial zones                                                                           |
| Landslides         | Limited          | Casile upland                                                                                            |

## Flood Risk by Zone

| Risk Level        | Areas                                                                                  |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Severe (Red)      | Marinig (all puroks), lakeshore barangays along Laguna de Bay                          |
| Moderate (Orange) | Parts of Barangay Diez, Nueve, Siete; low-lying interior roads near canals and esteros |
| Lower (Green)     | Elevated subdivisions, concrete-developed zones, Poblacion core                        |

> Flooding in moderate-risk areas occurs after **3–6 hours of heavy, continuous rainfall** as drainage overflows.

## Flood Evacuation — Step by Step

### Phase 1: Before the Disaster (Prep)

- Prepare your **go-bag** (water, food, flashlight, power bank, IDs, documents, medicines)
- Know your **primary evacuation center** (ask your Barangay Hall if unsure)
- Identify **two evacuation routes** from your home — choose which street floods first and which route is on higher ground
- Charge all devices; fill water containers when a typhoon signal is raised

### Phase 2: When a Warning Is Issued

- Monitor your **Barangay Facebook page**, **CDRRMO social media**, and barangay announcements
- If heavy rain or typhoon alert is raised: charge everything, move valuables to higher floors, and **be ready to leave immediately**
- Do not wait until floodwater is at your door — **evacuate early**

### Phase 3: During Flooding

**Do NOT:**

- Walk through moving or deep floodwater (swept-away deaths happen this way)
- Wait for rescue if you can still leave safely on your own

**Do:**

- Proceed to your nearest evacuation center immediately
- Stay updated through barangay announcements and CDRRMO social media

### Phase 4: If You Need Rescue

Call the **CDRRMO Rescue 911:**

- **0966-690-7561**
- **0995-672-8959**

When calling, state:

1. Your **exact location** (street name + barangay + nearest landmark)
2. **Number of people** at the location
3. Any **injuries or persons needing medical help**

> Rescue response time: ~5–15 minutes along main roads; 15–40 minutes in interior flooded zones where boats must be deployed.

### Phase 5: At the Evacuation Center

- **Register immediately** with the camp management team
- Secure your belongings
- Follow camp rules and instructions from CDRRMO and barangay officials
- Relief goods (food, water, hygiene kits) are pre-positioned at centers

### Phase 6: After the Disaster

- **Do not return home until officially cleared** by your Barangay Captain or CDRRMO
- Watch for: contaminated floodwater, fallen electrical lines, unstable structures
- Report damage to your Barangay Hall for LGU assessment and relief eligibility

## Earthquake Response (Drop, Cover, Hold On)

1. **Drop** to the ground
2. **Cover** your head and neck under a sturdy table or against an interior wall
3. **Hold on** until shaking stops
4. After shaking: exit the building calmly; stay away from damaged structures
5. Expect aftershocks — do not re-enter damaged buildings
