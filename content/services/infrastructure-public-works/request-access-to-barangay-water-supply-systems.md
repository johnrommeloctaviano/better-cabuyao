# Cabuyao City: Water Supply and Utility Access

Water, electricity, and telecommunications services in Cabuyao City are provided by a mix of government-regulated utilities and private service providers.

---

## Water Supply

Water service in Cabuyao is managed by local water districts and private utility concessionaires depending on your area.

**Common issues reported by residents:**

- Water pressure variability — particularly in newly developed subdivisions
- Service expansion lag in rapidly growing residential areas

**How to apply for a new water connection:**

1. Identify which water service provider covers your address (ask your barangay hall or neighbors).
2. Visit the nearest water district or utility office to apply for a service connection.
3. Submit requirements (valid ID, proof of address, lot/property documents).
4. Pay connection fees and wait for installation schedule.

**For water supply concerns (low pressure, service interruptions, no service):**

- Contact your water service provider directly.
- For unserved areas, raise the concern through your **Barangay Hall** — the barangay can escalate to the city government for infrastructure planning.

---

## Electricity

**Provider:** MERALCO (Manila Electric Company)

MERALCO serves the entire Cabuyao City area. Electrical infrastructure expansion is ongoing, tied to:

- Industrial growth (Pulo industrial area, Banay-Banay logistics corridor)
- New residential subdivisions

**For new electrical connections or service concerns:**

- Contact MERALCO directly via their hotline or online portal.
- For street lighting issues on **barangay or city roads**, report to the **City Engineering Office (CEO)**.
- For street lighting on **national roads and highways**, report to **DPWH**.

---

## Telecommunications

Fiber broadband and mobile network coverage in Cabuyao is expanding:

| Provider     | Service                        |
| ------------ | ------------------------------ |
| PLDT / Smart | Fiber broadband, LTE/5G mobile |
| Globe / TM   | Fiber broadband, LTE/5G mobile |

Cell tower expansion and fiber rollout are actively ongoing (2025–2026). For coverage issues or new connection requests, contact your preferred provider directly.

---

## Reporting Utility Infrastructure Issues to the LGU

While utilities are managed by private providers, the **City Engineering Office** can assist with:

- Coordinating streetlight repairs on local roads
- Endorsing unserved community areas to utility providers
- Flagging utility hazards (downed electrical lines, collapsed poles) to relevant agencies

Report urgent hazards to your **Barangay Hall** immediately, who will coordinate with the CEO and relevant agencies.

---

## Key Contacts

- **City Engineering Office (CEO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (street lighting, local road utilities)
- **Your Barangay Hall** — first point of contact for community utility concerns
- **MERALCO** — electricity connections and outages
- **DPWH Laguna 2nd District** — national road lighting
