# Cabuyao City: Zoning, Land Use, and Building Permits

Before constructing, renovating, or developing property in Cabuyao City, you must secure the appropriate permits from the **City Planning & Development Office (CPDO)** and the **Office of the Building Official (OBO)**.

---

## Cabuyao's Land Use Zones (CLUP Summary)

The city's **Comprehensive Land Use Plan (CLUP)** divides Cabuyao's land into four major policy areas:

| Zone                 | Coverage                                                        | Housing Allowed?               |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Settlement Areas     | ~25% (~1,079 ha) — residential subdivisions, urban barangays    | Yes                            |
| Production Areas     | ~39% — agriculture, industrial, economic zones                  | Restricted (rezoning required) |
| Protection Areas     | ~28.78% — watersheds, flood-prone zones, fault lines, lakeshore | **No** — strictly prohibited   |
| Infrastructure Areas | ~7% — roads, utilities, public facilities                       | No (public use)                |

---

## Where Building Is Prohibited

**No new construction is permitted in:**

| Restricted Area                         | Barangays Affected               |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Laguna de Bay shoreline (lake easement) | Bigaa, Butong, Gulod             |
| Riverbanks and estero easements         | Banlic, Mamatid                  |
| Landslide / fault line zones            | Casile, Diezmo, San Isidro       |
| Flood protection and watershed zones    | Per hazard map (check with CPDO) |

---

## Key Building and Development Rules

- **Setbacks** — minimum distances from roads, rivers, and boundaries must be observed
- **Height limits** — vary by zone classification
- **Density limits** — lot coverage and floor area ratios apply in residential zones
- **Mixed-use** — allowed in commercial zones (verify classification with CPDO)
- **Subdivision development** — requires a separate subdivision permit and environmental clearance

## Permits Required for Construction

| Permit                                     | Where to Get                              | Purpose                                       |
| ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Zoning Clearance                           | City Planning & Development Office (CPDO) | Confirms land use classification is compliant |
| Building Permit                            | Office of the Building Official (OBO)     | Required before any construction begins       |
| Sanitary Permit                            | City Health Office                        | For facilities with water/drainage systems    |
| Electrical Permit                          | OBO / Licensed Electrician                | For electrical installations                  |
| Occupancy Permit                           | OBO                                       | Required before occupying a new structure     |
| Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC) | DENR-EMB                                  | For larger developments                       |

---

## How to Apply for a Zoning Clearance

1. **Go to the CPDO** — City Planning & Development Office, Cabuyao City Hall.
2. **Request a zoning verification** for your property (bring your lot title or tax declaration).
3. The CPDO will confirm:
   - What zone your property falls under
   - What uses are permitted
   - Whether your planned development is allowed
4. If compliant, a **Zoning Clearance** will be issued.

## How to Apply for a Building Permit

1. Secure your **Zoning Clearance** from CPDO first.
2. Go to the **Office of the Building Official (OBO)**, Cabuyao City Hall.
3. Submit:
   - Accomplished application form
   - Lot title or tax declaration
   - Zoning Clearance
   - Building plans (signed by a licensed architect or engineer)
   - Proof of property ownership or authority to build
4. Pay fees and wait for permit issuance.
5. **Do not begin construction before the permit is issued** — doing so is a violation and can result in a stop-work order.

## Real Property Tax and Assessments

The **City Assessor's Office** handles:

- Property valuation (for tax purposes)
- Real Property Tax (RPT) records
- Required for land title transfers and loan collateral documentation

Pay annual Real Property Tax at the **City Treasurer's Office** (deadline: January 31 for discounts; March 31 for full payment).

---

## Key Contacts

- **City Planning & Development Office (CPDO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (zoning, CLUP, land use)
- **Office of the Building Official (OBO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (building permits)
- **City Assessor's Office** — Cabuyao City Hall (property valuation, RPT records)
- **DENR-EMB Region IV-A** — Environmental Compliance Certificate for large developments
- **Land Registration Authority (LRA)** — land title registration and verification
