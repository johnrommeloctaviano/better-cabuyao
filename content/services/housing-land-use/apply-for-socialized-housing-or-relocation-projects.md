# Cabuyao City: Apply for Socialized Housing or Relocation

The **City Urban Development & Housing Affairs Office (CUDHAO)** is Cabuyao City's primary housing authority. It manages socialized housing programs, informal settler relocation, and connects residents to national housing programs.

---

## Who Is Prioritized

| Priority Level                 | Who                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Highest (immediate relocation) | Families in flood-prone and danger zones (Bigaa, Butong, Gulod, Banlic riverbanks, Mamatid low-lying areas) |
| High                           | Informal settlers identified in city profiling (~723 households in danger areas)                            |
| Medium                         | Low-income renters and informal settlers in Sala, Marinig, Baclaran                                         |
| Standard                       | Low-income Cabuyao residents in stable areas                                                                |

## Housing Programs Available (2026)

### A. Socialized Housing (LGU-Coordinated)

- Government-built housing units or relocation sites
- Subsidized amortization (typically 20–30 year payment period)
- Managed through CUDHAO in coordination with the **National Housing Authority (NHA)** and **DHSUD**

### B. Informal Settler Resettlement

- For families residing in **danger zones** (flood-prone areas, riverbanks, lake easements)
- Off-site resettlement to safer housing areas
- In-city relocation where feasible

### C. Community Mortgage Program (CMP)

- Allows communities to **collectively purchase the land they occupy**
- Government-financed through the **Social Housing Finance Corporation (SHFC)**
- Ideal for organized communities with existing structures on privately owned land

### D. Expanded 4PH Program — "Pambansang Pabahay para sa Pilipino"

- National mid-rise housing program (2024–2026 active)
- Focus: workers, urban poor, relocation beneficiaries
- ECMP housing sites in Cabuyao participating in this program

### E. Local Shelter Plan (LSP)

- Cabuyao's city-level housing roadmap
- Prioritizes informal settlers, disaster-prone residents, and low-income earners

## How to Apply — Step by Step

1. **Go to the CUDHAO** — City Urban Development & Housing Affairs Office, Cabuyao City Hall.
2. **Register as a beneficiary** and request an application form.
3. **Submit the following documents** (typical requirements):
   - Valid ID (government-issued)
   - Proof of Cabuyao residency (barangay certificate or utility bill)
   - Barangay certification (indigency, residency, or endorsement)
   - Income certificate or proof of income level
   - Marriage certificate (if applicable)
   - Birth certificates of dependents
4. **Profiling and validation** — CUDHAO social workers assess your:
   - Income level
   - Current housing status
   - Risk exposure (danger zone residency)
5. **Program matching** — you are assigned to the appropriate housing program (relocation, CMP, 4PH, etc.)
6. **Approval and unit/lot awarding** — upon approval, you are assigned a housing unit or lot.
7. **Payment scheme** — subsidized amortization, typically 20–30 years.

## Hazard-Based Relocation Priority Areas

These barangays have residents prioritized for relocation due to flood and hazard risks:

| Priority           | Barangays                             | Reason                           |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| High (danger zone) | Bigaa, Butong, Gulod                  | Coastal lakeshore — flood-prone  |
| High (danger zone) | Banlic, Mamatid (low-lying)           | Riverbank easements — flood risk |
| Medium             | Sala (lower areas), Marinig, Baclaran | Moderate flood exposure          |

> Residents in **Protection Areas** (lake easements, riverbanks, fault lines) are **not legally permitted to remain** under the Comprehensive Land Use Plan. Relocation is required, not voluntary.
