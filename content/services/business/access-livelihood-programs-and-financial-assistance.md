# Cabuyao City: Livelihood Programs and Financial Assistance

Cabuyao City residents can access livelihood grants, starter kits, loans, and skills training through the LGU and partner national agencies. Programs reach all **18 barangays** and are deployed through barangay halls, community events, and targeted beneficiary lists.

---

## How Programs Are Distributed

Programs come from four main sources and are deployed **through your barangay**:

| Source                                         | Programs                                                                         |
| ---------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| LGU Cabuyao (PESO / CSWD / Agriculture Office) | City livelihood assistance, employment matching, agricultural support            |
| DOLE                                           | TUPAD, Integrated Livelihood Program (DILP), Government Internship Program (GIP) |
| DTI                                            | Livelihood Seeding Program, Negosyo Center training, BMBE                        |
| TESDA                                          | Skills training (bread & pastry, welding, tailoring, caregiving, electrical)     |
| DA (Dept. of Agriculture)                      | Seeds, urban gardening, livestock, farming support                               |

> Programs are **rotational** — not all programs are available at all times. Monitor your barangay's announcements and Facebook page.

---

## Key Programs Available to Cabuyao Residents

### DOLE TUPAD (Emergency Employment)

- Short-term community work (10–30 days)
- Tasks: cleaning, community improvement
- **Paid daily wage**
- Deployed per barangay batch

### DOLE Integrated Livelihood Program (DILP)

- Grants for livelihood starters: food carts, sari-sari stores, sewing kits
- No repayment required (grant-based)
- Applied for through your barangay or PESO Office

### DTI Livelihood Seeding Program (LSP)

- Starter kits for food processing, retail, and micro-enterprise
- Distributed during barangay caravans and LGU events
- Coordinate with the **Negosyo Center Cabuyao**

### LGU City Livelihood Assistance

- Cash or equipment support
- **Priority beneficiaries:** solo parents, PWDs, low-income families
- Apply at the **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)**

### P3 Loan (DTI — Pondo sa Pagbabago at Pag-asenso)

- **Micro-loans** for small businesses
- No collateral required
- Low interest rate
- Apply via the **Negosyo Center Cabuyao**

### SPES (Special Program for Employment of Students)

- Seasonal paid work for students (typically summer)
- Apply at the **PESO Office, Cabuyao City Hall**

## TESDA Skills Training → Livelihood

TESDA offers free technical-vocational training that can be the foundation for a livelihood:

| Course                      | Possible Livelihood              |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Bread and Pastry Production | Home bakery, food stall          |
| Tailoring / Dressmaking     | Sewing livelihood, alterations   |
| Electrical Installation     | Electrical services business     |
| Welding                     | Fabrication and repairs          |
| Caregiving / Housekeeping   | Employment or home-based service |

Training is offered at TESDA-accredited centers. Inquire at the **PESO Office** for referrals.

## Agriculture-Based Livelihood (Select Barangays)

Programs from the **City Agriculture Office** and **DA** are most active in:

**Bigaa, Banay-Banay, Gulod, Casile, San Isidro, Pulo**

Available support:

- Seed and seedling distribution
- Urban gardening kits
- Livestock support (poultry, swine)
- Coffee farming support
- Food processing training for farm produce

## How to Access Programs in Your Barangay

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall** — ask: _"May livelihood program po ba ngayon? Puwede po ba akong mag-apply?"_
2. **Visit the PESO Office** at Cabuyao City Hall for employment and livelihood matching.
3. **Go to the Negosyo Center Cabuyao** for DTI programs, training schedules, and loan applications.
4. **Monitor barangay Facebook pages** — announcements are posted there first.

---

## Key Contacts

- **Negosyo Center Cabuyao** — DTI programs, loans, training
- **PESO Office, Cabuyao City Hall** — employment + livelihood matching, SPES
- **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)** — LGU assistance, PWD, solo parent programs
- **City Agriculture Office** — farming and agri-based livelihood
