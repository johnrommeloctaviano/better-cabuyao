# Cabuyao City: Relief Goods and Cash Aid During Disasters

During emergencies — typhoons, fires, flooding, and other disasters — the **City Government of Cabuyao** coordinates relief operations through the **CSWDO**, the **City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)**, and your **Barangay Hall**.

---

## How Relief Distribution Works

Barangays serve as the **primary distribution point** for relief goods and emergency aid. The process flows from national/city agencies down to your barangay.

| Level                      | Role                                                                    |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| DSWD / National Government | Releases national relief goods and funding                              |
| CSWDO (City Level)         | Coordinates, approves cash assistance, manages case assessment          |
| Barangay (BSWO)            | Distributes relief goods, screens beneficiaries, handles initial intake |

---

## Types of Assistance Available

| Assistance                                 | Details                                           |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Relief goods (food packs)                  | Rice, canned goods, and basic necessities         |
| Emergency cash assistance                  | For fire victims, disaster-affected families      |
| Temporary shelter support                  | Evacuation center coordination and referral       |
| AICS (Assistance to Individuals in Crisis) | Financial, medical, burial, or transportation aid |

---

## Who Can Receive Relief

Priority beneficiaries during disasters:

- Fire victims
- Flood / typhoon-affected families
- Accident victims
- Families with sudden loss of income due to disaster

---

## How to Receive Relief Goods (Barangay Level)

1. **Stay in contact with your Barangay Hall** — relief distribution announcements are made through barangay officials, Facebook pages, and local speakers.
2. **Report your situation** to the Barangay Captain or Barangay Social Welfare Officer (BSWO).
3. **Present a valid ID** and be listed in the barangay's affected families roster.
4. **Collect relief goods** at the barangay hall, covered court, or designated distribution point.

---

## How to Apply for Emergency Cash Assistance (CSWDO / AICS)

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall** immediately after the incident.
   - Request: Certificate of Indigency + incident certification from barangay officials.
2. **Bring the following to the CSWDO Office, Cabuyao City Hall:**
   - Valid ID
   - Barangay indigency certificate
   - Incident report (from barangay or fire station, if applicable)
   - Proof of loss (photos, billing statements, etc.)
3. **Social worker assessment** — your case will be evaluated for urgency and eligibility.
4. **Receive assistance** — urgent cases may be processed same day.

---

## Malasakit Center (For Medical Emergencies)

For disaster victims needing hospital care, **Malasakit Centers** (available in partner hospitals) combine DSWD, PhilHealth, DOH, and PCSO assistance in one counter — often faster than going directly to CSWDO.

---

## Pro Tips

- **Go to your Barangay Hall first** — barangay endorsement speeds up CSWDO processing.
- **Complete documents = faster approval.** Missing an incident report or barangay certificate is the most common cause of delays.
- **Not all assistance is cash** — you may receive a guarantee letter, partial aid, or referral to a partner agency instead.

---

## Key Contacts

- **Barangay Hall / BSWO** — first point of contact for relief
- **CSWDO, Cabuyao City Hall** — cash and crisis assistance
- **City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)** — disaster response coordination
