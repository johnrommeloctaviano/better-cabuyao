# Cabuyao City: Livelihood and Skills Training Programs (Social Welfare)

The **CSWDO** provides livelihood assistance as a core social welfare service, targeting the city's most vulnerable residents — indigent families, PWDs, solo parents, and 4Ps beneficiaries.

> For the full livelihood and business program guide (including DTI, DOLE, TESDA, and Negosyo Center programs), see **Business and Livelihood > Access Livelihood Programs and Financial Assistance**.

---

## Who Is Prioritized

| Group                                                   | Notes                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Indigent families                                       | Primary beneficiaries of CSWDO livelihood programs |
| 4Ps (Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program) beneficiaries | Often given priority slots                         |
| Solo parents                                            | Priority under Solo Parent Welfare Act             |
| PWDs                                                    | Livelihood support as part of PWD assistance       |
| Unemployed individuals                                  | Referred through PESO Office                       |

---

## Types of Livelihood Support

### Starter Kits / Capital Assistance

- Equipment or starter materials for small livelihood ventures (sewing kits, food processing, retail)
- Distributed through **CSWDO** in coordination with DTI and DOLE

### Skills Training Programs

- Practical skills for employment or self-employment
- Often conducted at barangay halls or covered courts
- Common training areas: bread and pastry, tailoring, food processing, urban gardening

### Community Livelihood Projects

- Group-based livelihood activities organized at the barangay level
- Facilitated by **Barangay Social Welfare Officers (BSWOs)**

### Job Readiness Programs

- Resume writing, interview skills, workplace readiness
- Coordinated with the **PESO Office, Cabuyao City Hall**

## How to Apply

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall** — ask the BSWO about current or upcoming livelihood programs and enrollment.
2. **Register your intent** — some programs require advance enrollment or beneficiary listing.
3. **Submit requirements** (typical):
   - Barangay Certificate of Indigency
   - Valid ID
   - Proof of eligibility (4Ps, Solo Parent ID, PWD ID, etc. as applicable)
4. **Attend required training** — many programs require participation before kits or capital are released.
5. **Receive assistance** — kits, training certification, or referral to partner programs.

---

## Reality Check

- Programs are **rotational** — not all are available at the same time. Monitor your barangay's announcements.
- Slots are **limited** — apply as early as possible when programs open.
- Training attendance is often a **prerequisite** for receiving a starter kit or capital assistance.
