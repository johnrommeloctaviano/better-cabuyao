# Cabuyao City: Family and Community Welfare Support

The **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)** provides a range of services for families, children, women, youth, and individuals in difficult situations. These services are delivered at both the city (CSWDO) and barangay (BSWO) level.

---

## Services Available

### Family Welfare

| Service                        | Details                                                              |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Family counseling              | Professional counseling for family conflicts, separation, and crisis |
| Parenting seminars             | Guidance for parents, especially for families with young children    |
| Domestic violence intervention | Support and legal referral for abuse cases                           |
| Case management                | Ongoing support for abused, neglected, and at-risk families          |

### Child Welfare

| Service                    | Details                                                         |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Child protection services  | Response to cases of abuse, exploitation, and neglect           |
| Youth development programs | Leadership, life skills, and community engagement               |
| ECCD / Daycare services    | Early childhood education at barangay level (see Daycare guide) |
| Supplementary feeding      | Nutrition programs for at-risk children                         |

### Women Protection (VAWC Services)

| Service                                            | Details                                                                   |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| VAWC (Violence Against Women and Children) support | Intake, assessment, and crisis response                                   |
| Legal referrals                                    | Referral to the Public Attorney's Office (PAO) and family courts          |
| Psychosocial counseling                            | Trauma-informed counseling by CSWDO social workers                        |
| Shelter / referral services                        | Access to women's shelters and protective custody (coordinated with DSWD) |

### Special Protection Programs

| Service                                  | Who It Serves                                 |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Children in conflict with the law (CICL) | Legal support, diversion, and reintegration   |
| Victims of trafficking                   | Recovery, reintegration, and legal assistance |
| Abandoned individuals                    | Emergency support and case management         |
| Street children                          | Outreach and reintegration programs           |

### Financial Assistance (AICS)

For individuals and families in crisis:

| Type                      | Examples                                             |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Medical assistance        | Hospital bills, medicine, procedures                 |
| Burial assistance         | Death of a family member (especially indigent cases) |
| Transportation assistance | Travel for medical treatment or repatriation         |
| Food / relief goods       | Emergency food packs                                 |
| Educational assistance    | Select cases with urgent need                        |

## How to Access Services

### At Your Barangay Hall (BSWO — First Stop)

- The **Barangay Social Welfare Officer (BSWO)** handles initial screening and minor cases
- Issues: Certificate of Indigency, referral letters to CSWDO
- Handles: barangay-level family disputes, initial crisis intake

### At the CSWDO Office (City Hall — For Major Cases)

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall first** — get a Certificate of Indigency and/or referral letter.
2. **Bring your documents:**
   - Valid ID
   - Barangay Certificate (indigency or residency)
   - Supporting documents (medical bills, incident report, birth certificate, etc.)
3. **Social worker intake and assessment** — your case will be assigned to a social worker.
4. **Receive assistance or referral** — may be same-day (urgent cases) or within a few days.

### For Medical Cases — Malasakit Center (Faster)

If you need medical financial assistance, go to the **Malasakit Center** at your hospital. It combines DSWD, PhilHealth, DOH, and PCSO support at one counter — often faster than going directly to the CSWDO.

---

## Important Notes

- **Barangay endorsement matters** — a referral letter from your barangay increases your approval chances.
- **Complete documents are critical** — missing paperwork is the most common cause of delay or rejection.
- **Go early** — arrive before 8:00 AM at CSWDO; avoid Mondays and post-disaster days.
- **Be honest in your interview** — social workers verify your situation; exaggeration can result in rejection.

## Key Contacts

- **CSWDO, Cabuyao City Hall** — all major welfare services
- **Your Barangay Hall (BSWO)** — first point of contact, certificates, referrals
- **Philippine National Police (PNP) – Women's Desk** — VAWC incident reporting
- **Public Attorney's Office (PAO)** — free legal aid for qualified residents
