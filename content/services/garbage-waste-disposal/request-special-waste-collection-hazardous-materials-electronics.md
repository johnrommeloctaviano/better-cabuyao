# Cabuyao City: Special Waste Collection — Bulk, Hazardous, and E-Waste

Regular garbage collection trucks handle **ordinary household waste**. For bulk items, hazardous materials, and electronic waste, a different process applies.

---

## Bulk Waste (Furniture, Large Items, Construction Debris)

For large items that don't fit in regular bins — old furniture, broken appliances, construction rubble:

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall** and request a special collection schedule.
2. The barangay coordinates with the **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** or **General Services Office (GSO)** for a separate hauling arrangement.
3. Prepare your items at the designated location and time advised by the barangay.

> **Construction and demolition waste** (concrete, sand, steel) requires separate disposal arrangements — confirm with CENRO whether the city handles it or if you must arrange a private hauler.

---

## Hazardous Household Waste

These items **cannot go into regular garbage bins**:

| Item                                | Why It's Hazardous | Disposal Option                                             |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Batteries (dry cell, car batteries) | Heavy metals       | Bring to designated drop-off or e-waste drive               |
| Fluorescent bulbs / CFLs            | Mercury            | Bring to CENRO or hardware store take-back                  |
| Paint, solvents, chemicals          | Toxic              | Keep sealed; inquire at CENRO for disposal events           |
| Pesticides / insecticides           | Toxic              | Do not pour down drains; inquire at City Agriculture Office |

> Hazardous waste is **not regularly collected** through regular routes. Improper disposal is an environmental violation under RA 9003.

---

## Electronic Waste (E-Waste)

Old phones, computers, printers, chargers, and other electronics contain hazardous components and must not go in regular garbage.

**Options in Cabuyao:**

1. **LGU e-waste collection drives** — CENRO periodically organizes e-waste collection events. Monitor the **Cabuyao City LGU Facebook page** and barangay announcements.
2. **Manufacturer take-back programs** — Many electronics brands (Samsung, PLDT, Globe, etc.) have drop-off points or take-back events.
3. **Accredited e-waste collectors** — DENR-accredited e-waste recyclers accept devices. Inquire at CENRO for a current referral list.

---

## Medical / Clinical Waste (Household)

For used syringes, medical sharps, expired medicines, or bandages from home care:

- **Used sharps (needles):** Place in a hard container (e.g., a sealed plastic bottle), label it clearly, and bring to your nearest **City Health Office (CHO)** or **Cabuyao City Hospital** for proper disposal.
- **Expired medicines:** Do not flush down toilets or throw in regular trash. Bring to CHO I or CHO II for disposal guidance.

---

## How to Report Improper Disposal

If you witness illegal dumping or improper waste disposal:

1. Document the incident (photo / location / time) if safe to do so.
2. Report to your **Barangay Hall** (Barangay Captain or BSWO).
3. File a formal complaint at the **CENRO, Cabuyao City Hall**.
4. You may also report to the **City Environmental Enforcement** unit.

---

## Key Contacts

- **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (special collections, e-waste drives, hazardous waste)
- **General Services Office (GSO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (bulk waste hauling coordination)
- **Your Barangay Hall** — first point of contact for special collection requests
- **City Health Office I or II** — medical waste disposal guidance
