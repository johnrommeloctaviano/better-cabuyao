# Cabuyao City: Proper Waste Segregation and Disposal

Waste segregation is **mandatory** in Cabuyao City under **Republic Act 9003 (Ecological Solid Waste Management Act)**. Proper segregation reduces landfill waste, prevents flooding, protects public health, and supports Cabuyao's recycling programs.

---

## Waste Categories

Sort your waste into these categories before collection day:

| Category                         | What Goes Here                                                     | Collection Day                              |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Biodegradable**                | Food waste, vegetable peels, garden trimmings, leaves              | Per barangay schedule (typically 2x/week)   |
| **Non-biodegradable / Residual** | Plastic bags, sachets, styrofoam, candy wrappers, soiled packaging | Per barangay schedule (1–2x/week)           |
| **Recyclables**                  | Clean bottles (glass/plastic), metals, cardboard, paper, tin cans  | Separate day or via barangay MRF / junkshop |
| **Special / Hazardous**          | Batteries, electronics, medical waste, chemicals                   | Special disposal (see below)                |

---

## Practical Segregation Tips

- Use **separate bins or bags** for each category — color-coding helps (green = biodegradable, black = residual, blue = recyclable).
- **Rinse** recyclables before sorting — dirty recyclables are treated as residual waste.
- **Flatten** cardboard and plastic bottles to save space.
- Do **not** mix segregated waste with unsegregated waste — this defeats the purpose.
- Put waste out **before 5:30 AM** on your collection day (morning shift starts early).

---

## Materials Recovery Facilities (MRFs)

Cabuyao barangays operate **MRFs** to sort recyclables, recover usable materials, and reduce landfill load.

| MRF Status                                    | Barangays                                  |
| --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Fully functional / active                     | Mamatid, Marinig, Pulo, Niugan             |
| Partially active (composting + basic sorting) | Baclaran, Banay-Banay, Bigaa, Gulod        |
| Cluster-based / shared access                 | Casile, Pittland, Diezmo, San Isidro, Sala |

You can **bring clean recyclables directly to your barangay MRF** on sorting days. Ask your Barangay Hall for the MRF schedule and location.

---

## Composting

Cabuyao encourages composting at the household and barangay level:

- **Household composting:** Compost food scraps (vegetable peels, fruit waste, coffee grounds) in a bin or pit at home.
- **Barangay compost pits:** Some barangays operate community compost areas — ask your BSWO or Barangay Hall.
- Finished compost can be used for home gardening or donated to the City Agriculture Office's urban farming programs.

---

## Where Garbage Goes After Collection

1. Collected waste is hauled by the city's contracted garbage collection service.
2. Recyclables diverted at MRFs are sorted and sent to junkshop buyers or recycling partners.
3. Residual and non-recyclable waste is sent to a **sanitary landfill** (per RA 9003 — open dumping is illegal).
4. Organic waste in some barangays goes to composting facilities.

---

## Reality Check

- Segregation is **legally required** but compliance is inconsistent across barangays.
- Some collection trucks still mix waste after pickup — this is a known gap being addressed.
- **The most impactful action** you can take: segregate correctly at home and encourage your neighbors to do the same.
