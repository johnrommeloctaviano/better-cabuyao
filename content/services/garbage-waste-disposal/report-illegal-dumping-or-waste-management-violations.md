# Cabuyao City: Report Illegal Dumping and Waste Management Violations

Illegal dumping, open burning of waste, and mixing of segregated garbage are violations under **Republic Act 9003** and local Cabuyao ordinances. Residents are encouraged to report violations to help keep the city clean.

---

## What Counts as a Violation

| Violation                            | Examples                                                             |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Illegal dumping                      | Throwing garbage on vacant lots, roadsides, canals, or the lakeshore |
| Open burning of waste                | Burning garbage, leaves, or plastic in residential areas             |
| Mixed waste disposal                 | Knowingly mixing segregated categories at collection points          |
| Improper disposal of hazardous waste | Leaving batteries, chemicals, or e-waste in regular bins             |
| Improper waste storage               | Allowing garbage to accumulate and create health or flood hazards    |

---

## How to Report

**Step 1: Document the violation (if safe)**

- Take a photo of the dumping site or burning, noting the location, date, and time.

**Step 2: Report to Your Barangay Hall**

- Inform the **Barangay Captain**, **Barangay Secretary**, or **Barangay Environmental Coordinator**.
- Barangays are empowered to issue notices and take initial corrective action.

**Step 3: File a Formal Complaint at CENRO**

- For serious or recurring violations, file a complaint at the **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO), Cabuyao City Hall**.
- Bring: your report, any documentation (photos/video), and your valid ID.

---

## What Happens After You Report

1. The barangay or CENRO issues a notice to the violator.
2. For serious cases, the CENRO can coordinate with the **City Environmental Enforcement** unit.
3. Repeat violators or serious dumping cases may be subject to fines and penalties under RA 9003 and local ordinances.

---

## Canal and Drainage Clogging

If illegal dumping is causing canal blockage or flooding risk:

- Report to your **Barangay Hall** and the **City Engineering Office (CEO)** simultaneously.
- CEO handles drainage clearing; CENRO handles the waste violation.

---

## Community Clean-Up Drives

You can also help proactively:

- **Join barangay clean-up drives** — organized regularly, especially before rainy season and after typhoons.
- **Participate in CENRO-organized community programs** — environmental clean-ups and awareness campaigns.
- Monitor the **Cabuyao City LGU Facebook page** for clean-up event announcements.

---

## Key Contacts

- **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (violations, complaints, clean-up programs)
- **Your Barangay Hall** — initial reporting and barangay-level enforcement
- **City Engineering Office (CEO)** — for drainage issues caused by illegal dumping
