# Cabuyao City: Free Seeds, Seedlings, Fertilizers, and Fingerlings

The **City Agriculture Office (CAO)** distributes free and subsidized agricultural inputs to registered farmers and fisherfolk in Cabuyao City. Registration in the appropriate registry is required to access these benefits.

---

## Who Can Apply

| Beneficiary | Requirement                                                                    |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Farmers     | Registered in the **RSBSA** (Registry System for Basic Sectors in Agriculture) |
| Fisherfolk  | Registered in the **Municipal Fisherfolk Registry**                            |
| Both        | Resident of Cabuyao City; actively farming or fishing                          |

> **Registration is the key.** Unregistered farmers and fisherfolk cannot access government subsidies, seeds, or fingerlings. Register at the City Agriculture Office first.

---

## Agricultural Inputs Available

### Seeds and Seedlings

- **Rice seeds** — distributed seasonally in alignment with planting cycles
- **Vegetable seedlings** — pechay, eggplant, tomato, and other high-value crops
- **Fruit tree seedlings** — banana, papaya, and other varieties
- **Organic crop seeds** — under the organic agriculture program

### Fertilizers

- Inorganic fertilizers (limited, based on program availability)
- **Organic fertilizers** — compost, vermicast (paired with organic farming training)

### Backyard / Urban Farming Kits

- Container gardening kits
- Vertical gardening materials
- Composting training and materials
- Under the **National Urban Agriculture Program (NUAP)**

---

## Fingerlings (For Fisherfolk and Aquaculture)

| Species                       | Use                             |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Tilapia fingerlings           | Fish cage and fishpond stocking |
| Bangus (milkfish) fingerlings | Aquaculture / fishpond          |

Fingerlings are distributed in coordination with the **Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR)** and **DA Region IV-A**.

---

## Active Agricultural Barangays

Inputs are prioritized in active farming and fishing barangays:

| Zone                  | Barangays                                        | Type                      |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Lakeshore (Fisheries) | Bigaa, Butong, Marinig, Gulod, Baclaran, Mamatid | Fishing + mixed farming   |
| Agriculture Belt      | Banay-Banay, Niugan, Sala, San Isidro, Pulo      | Rice, corn, vegetables    |
| Upland                | Casile                                           | Diversified / fruit crops |

> Urban and densely populated barangays (Banlic, Diezmo, Pittland, Poblacion I–III) have minimal to no agri-fishery activity.

---

## How to Apply

1. **Register first** — go to the City Agriculture Office with a valid ID and barangay certificate.
   - Farmers: enroll in **RSBSA**
   - Fisherfolk: enroll in the **Municipal Fisherfolk Registry**
2. **Join a farmers or fisherfolk association** — membership increases priority access to inputs.
3. **Attend the required training** — disbursement is often linked to training participation.
4. **Apply during distribution windows** — the CAO announces input distribution schedules seasonally. Monitor your barangay's announcements and the City Agriculture Office.
5. **Receive your inputs** at the distribution point (barangay hall or CAO office).
