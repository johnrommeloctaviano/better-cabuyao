# Cabuyao City: Farm Tools, Equipment, and Fisheries Gear Assistance

The **City Agriculture Office (CAO)** provides access to farm machinery, tools, and fisheries gear to registered farmers and fisherfolk in Cabuyao City — reducing startup costs for small-scale agricultural producers.

---

## Farm Machinery and Equipment

Available through DA mechanization programs and the city's agriculture office:

| Equipment               | Use                                           |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Hand tractors (tillers) | Land preparation for rice and vegetable farms |
| Power tillers           | Soil cultivation                              |
| Irrigation pumps        | Water management for farmed plots             |
| Post-harvest equipment  | Threshers, dryers (availability varies)       |

**Access model:** Equipment may be borrowed, rented at subsidized rates, or accessed through a cooperative/association that owns shared machinery. Confirm current availability and terms at the CAO.

---

## Fisheries Gear Assistance

For registered fisherfolk:

| Gear / Item     | Notes                                              |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Fishing nets    | Distributed under gear assistance programs         |
| Fish cages      | For aquaculture (tilapia, bangus) in Laguna de Bay |
| Boats           | Select programs (limited availability)             |
| Fuel assistance | Available when program is active                   |

> **Marinig** is the main fisheries hub with a fishing port (major landing area). Gear programs are most active in lakeshore barangays.

---

## Shared Service Facilities (SSF)

Through DA and DTI programs, farmers and fisherfolk may access:

- **Food processing equipment** — for value-added products (dried fish, smoked fish, vegetable processing)
- **Packaging equipment** — for market-ready products
- **Fabrication tools** — for farm infrastructure

These are intended to reduce upfront capital requirements for MSMEs and agri-based livelihoods.

---

## Who Can Apply

- Resident of Cabuyao City
- Registered in **RSBSA** (farmers) or **Municipal Fisherfolk Registry** (fisherfolk)
- Member of a farmers association or fisherfolk organization _(recommended — groups have priority access)_
- Has completed relevant training for the requested equipment or program

| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |

| Agriculture Belt      | Banay-Banay, Niugan, Sala, San Isidro, Pulo      | Tractors, tillers, irrigation pumps     |
| Upland / Diversified  | Casile                                           | Specialized tools for fruit/mixed crops |

---

## How to Apply

1. **Register** at the City Agriculture Office (RSBSA or Fisherfolk Registry) if not yet registered.
2. **Join a farmers or fisherfolk association** — organized groups get priority access to shared equipment.
3. **Visit the City Agriculture Office (CAO)** and inquire about available equipment, current program terms, and application requirements.
4. **Attend required training** — some equipment programs require prior training participation.
5. **Submit application** with valid ID, barangay certificate, and association membership proof.
