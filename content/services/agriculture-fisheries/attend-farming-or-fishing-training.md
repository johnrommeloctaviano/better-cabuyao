# Cabuyao City: Farming and Fishing Training Programs

The **City Agriculture Office (CAO)** conducts regular training and extension services for farmers and fisherfolk in Cabuyao City, in partnership with **DA Region IV-A** and other agencies.

---

## Who Can Attend

- Registered farmers (RSBSA) and fisherfolk (Municipal Fisherfolk Registry)
- Members of farmers or fisherfolk associations / cooperatives
- Residents with active farming or fishing livelihoods

> Training is often a **prerequisite** for receiving seeds, equipment, or livelihood grants. Attending regularly also increases your priority for future programs.

---

## Regular Training Topics

### Farming Trainings

| Topic                         | Focus                                                              |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Modern farming techniques     | Improved rice, vegetable, and crop production methods              |
| High-value crops program      | Vegetables (pechay, eggplant, tomato), fruit trees, market linkage |
| Organic agriculture           | Organic fertilizers, natural pest control, certification guidance  |
| Climate-resilient agriculture | Flood/drought-tolerant varieties, adaptive farming practices       |
| Pest and disease management   | Integrated pest management, early detection, organic solutions     |
| Post-harvest handling         | Proper storage, grading, and processing of farm produce            |
| Urban and backyard farming    | Container farming, vertical gardening, composting (NUAP program)   |

### Fisheries Trainings

| Topic                               | Focus                                                        |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Aquaculture management              | Fish cage and fishpond operation for tilapia and bangus      |
| Fish feeding and disease management | Nutrition, disease prevention, and treatment                 |
| Fish processing                     | Daing (dried fish), smoked fish, and value-added products    |
| Lake management awareness           | Illegal fishing regulations, fish kill response, FARMC roles |

### Livelihood-Linked Trainings

| Topic                | Output                                 |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Mushroom production  | Livelihood starter kit upon completion |
| Hydroponics          | Urban farming livelihood               |
| Vegetable processing | Value-added agri products              |

## How to Join

1. **Register at the City Agriculture Office (CAO)** — or contact your Barangay Agriculture Technician (BAT).
2. **Join a farmers or fisherfolk association** — organized groups receive priority scheduling for training.
3. **Monitor announcements** — training schedules are announced at the CAO, barangay halls, and local Facebook pages.
4. **Attend and complete training** — attendance is often required to qualify for subsequent input distribution or equipment loans.

---

## Barangay Coverage

Training sessions are conducted at:

- The **City Agriculture Office**
- **Barangay halls** (for community-based sessions)
- **Covered courts** (for larger group sessions)

Most active in farming and fisheries zones: Bigaa, Butong, Gulod, Marinig, Baclaran, Mamatid, Banay-Banay, Niugan, Sala, San Isidro, Pulo, Casile.
