# Cabuyao City: Free Check-ups, Medicines, and Vaccines

The City Government of Cabuyao provides free **medical check-ups, basic medicines, and vaccines** through two City Health Offices (CHO I and CHO II) and Barangay Health Centers (BHCs) across all 18 barangays.

---

## How the System Works

Cabuyao operates a **tiered healthcare system**. Start at your Barangay Health Center — you will be referred up if needed.

| Level    | Facility                         | Best For                                        |
| -------- | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| First    | Barangay Health Center (BHC)     | Initial consultation, BHW assistance, screening |
| Second   | City Health Office I or II (CHO) | Full primary care, specialized programs         |
| Referral | Cabuyao City Hospital (CCH)      | Emergency, surgery, inpatient care              |

---

## City Health Office I (CHO I)

📍 **Location:** Sala / Poblacion, Cabuyao City

**Services**

- Free general consultations (OPD / walk-in)
- Basic medicines
- Immunization (EPI vaccines for children and adults)
- Dental services (extraction, basic consultation)
- Family planning and reproductive health
- Maternal and child care (prenatal, postnatal)
- Nutrition programs / Operation Timbang
- Medical certificate issuance

---

## City Health Office II (CHO II)

📍 **Location:** Brgy. Pulo, National Highway, Cabuyao City

**Services**

- All basic CHO services (same as CHO I)
- **TB-DOTS** treatment program (PhilHealth-accredited iDOTS facility)
- **Animal Bite Treatment Center** — anti-rabies vaccines, first dose free
- **YAKAP Mental Health Program** (PhilHealth-accredited until December 2026)

> CHO II is Cabuyao's **disease control and specialized referral hub** for the barangay health center network.

## Free Vaccines & Immunization

| Beneficiary        | Vaccine / Service                                 | Where                                 | Requirements                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Infants & children | BCG, Pentavalent, OPV/IPV, MMR, Hepatitis B (EPI) | Barangay Health Centers / CHO I       | Health record or ECCD card (issued onsite) |
| Pregnant women     | Tetanus Toxoid + supplements                      | Barangay Health Centers               | Prenatal record                            |
| Animal bite cases  | Anti-rabies vaccine (first dose free)             | CHO II – Animal Bite Treatment Center | Proof of Cabuyao residency                 |
| All residents      | COVID-19 (if ongoing)                             | CHO I or CHO II                       | Valid ID                                   |

## Free Basic Medicines

- Available at CHO I and CHO II pharmacies
- **Priority beneficiaries:** Senior citizens, indigent patients
- Under Universal Health Care (UHC), many medicines are **free or subsidized**
- Bring a doctor's prescription or referral

> **Note:** Medicine availability may vary. Shortages can occur during high-demand periods.

## Barangay Health Centers (BHCs)

Each of Cabuyao's **18 barangays** has a BHC as the first point of contact. Key confirmed-active facilities:

| Area                | Facility                                                                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Central / Poblacion | Brgy. 2 Health Center, City Health Office I (central hub)                                     |
| South / Highway     | Barangay Banlic Health Center, Banaybanay Barangay Health Station, Brgy. Diezmo Health Center |
| East                | Mamatid Health Center, Cabuyao 2 Super Health Center                                          |
| North               | Brgy. Gulod Health Center, San Isidro Health Center                                           |

> Not all BHCs are publicly listed. Some operate inside barangay halls or on a scheduled basis. Ask your barangay officials for the location of your nearest health center.

---

## How to Avail

1. **Walk in** to your Barangay Health Center or nearest CHO during operating hours.
2. **Register** — first-time patients will be issued a health record card.
3. **Consult** with the midwife, nurse, or physician on duty.
4. **Receive treatment, prescription, or referral** as appropriate.
5. **Claim medicines** at the CHO pharmacy window with your prescription.
