# Cabuyao City: Health Programs — Nutrition, Dengue Control, TB Treatment, and More

Cabuyao City implements several national and local health programs through **City Health Office I**, **City Health Office II**, and **Barangay Health Centers**. Most programs are **free** and open to all residents.

---

## 1. Nutrition Programs

**What's offered**

- **Operation Timbang** — barangay-wide weighing to detect and address child undernutrition
- **Supplementary feeding** — for malnourished children, coordinated with Barangay Nutrition Scholars (BNS)
- **Breastfeeding promotion** and health education for mothers
- Community nutrition activities during **Nutrition Month (July)**

**Who can join**

- Children under 5 and their families
- At-risk and malnourished individuals identified by the barangay

**How to join**

1. Visit your **Barangay Health Center** or ask the **Barangay Nutrition Scholar (BNS)** about feeding programs.
2. Attend barangay weighing events (Operation Timbang) — announced by your barangay.

**Cost:** Free

---

## 2. Dengue Prevention Programs

**What's offered**

- Community clean-up drives under **ABKD (Aksyon Barangay Kontra Dengue)**
- **4 o'clock habit** — daily search and destruction of mosquito breeding sites
- Misting and fogging by CHO, especially during rainy season
- Health information campaigns

**How to participate**

1. Join barangay clean-up drives and practice the **4S strategy**: Search and Destroy, Self-protection, Seek early consultation, Support misting/fogging.
2. Cooperate with CHO teams during house-to-house larval surveys.

**Cost:** Free — no registration required

---

## 3. TB-DOTS (Tuberculosis Treatment) — CHO II

City Health Office II is a **PhilHealth-accredited iDOTS facility** implementing the National Tuberculosis Program (NTP).

**What's offered**

- Free TB screening and diagnosis
- Full course of anti-TB medicines under **Directly Observed Therapy Short-course (DOTS)**
- Regular monitoring and follow-up care

**Where:** City Health Office II — Brgy. Pulo, National Highway, Cabuyao City

**How to join**

1. Go to **CHO II** or your Barangay Health Center for TB screening.
2. If diagnosed, you will be enrolled in DOTS — medicines and treatment are provided free.
3. Attend regular follow-up visits as scheduled by your treatment supervisor.

**Common TB symptoms to watch for:** Cough lasting 2 or more weeks, unexplained weight loss, night sweats, low-grade fever.

**Cost:** Free for all TB patients

---

## 4. Leptospirosis Response

**What's offered**

- Awareness campaigns, especially after flooding events
- Referral to CHO or hospital for early treatment

**How to avail:** Visit your BHC or CHO I/II if you experience symptoms (fever, muscle pain, red eyes) after contact with floodwater or soil.

---

## 5. HIV/AIDS Awareness and Testing

**What's offered**

- Awareness and education campaigns
- Limited testing (typically referred to designated accredited facilities)

**How to avail:** Inquire at CHO I or CHO II for referral to an accredited HIV testing center.

---

## 6. School-Based Health Programs

The CHO coordinates with schools to provide:

- Student health assessments
- Vaccination drives
- Dental missions

Implemented through the **Department of Education (DepEd)** and local health offices.

---

## 7. Universal Health Care (UHC) and PhilHealth

All CHOs and Barangay Health Centers participate in the **Universal Health Care Act**. Many services are:

- **Free** at the point of care
- Covered under **PhilHealth** and other government health programs

Bring your **PhilHealth ID** when visiting any health facility.
