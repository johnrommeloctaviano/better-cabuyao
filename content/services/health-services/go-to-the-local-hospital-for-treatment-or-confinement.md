# Cabuyao City: Local Hospital — Treatment and Confinement

For emergency cases, surgery, and inpatient care, residents are referred to **Cabuyao City Hospital (CCH)** — also known as **Ospital ng Cabuyao** — the main public referral hospital in the city.

---

## How the Referral System Works

Cabuyao follows a tiered care model. The hospital handles what your Barangay Health Center or CHO cannot.

| Tier      | Where                            | Handles                                   |
| --------- | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Primary   | Barangay Health Center (BHC)     | Screening, minor care, referrals          |
| Secondary | City Health Office I or II (CHO) | Full primary care, disease programs       |
| Referral  | Cabuyao City Hospital (CCH)      | Emergency, surgery, inpatient confinement |

> Go to your BHC or CHO first. If your condition requires hospital care, you will receive a referral to CCH.

---

## Cabuyao City Hospital (CCH) — Government Hospital

Also known as **Ospital ng Cabuyao**.

**Services**

- Emergency care — 24/7
- Inpatient (ward) confinement
- Surgery
- Out-patient consultations
- Referral cases from CHOs and BHCs

---

## Emergency — Quick Steps

| Step | What You Do                              | Hospital Does                           |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Go directly to the Emergency Room (24/7) | Triage, vital signs, initial orders     |
| 2    | Sign consent forms                       | Stabilization, tests, medication orders |
| 3    | Transfer to ward if for admission        | ER endorses to ward nurse on duty       |

**For emergencies, go straight to the ER — no referral needed.**

---

## PhilHealth & Free Services

- Cabuyao City Hospital is **PhilHealth-accredited**
- Many services are **free or covered under Universal Health Care (UHC)**
- **No Balance Billing (NBB)** applies for indigent, sponsored, and senior citizen patients under PhilHealth packages
- If PhilHealth documents are unavailable at admission, hospital staff can assist with follow-up document submission

---

## What to Bring

- Valid ID (with photo and signature)
- PhilHealth ID and MDR (Member Data Record)
- Proof of dependency (if enrolling as a dependent)
- Doctor's referral or admitting order (for elective / planned admission)
- HMO / company guarantee letter (if applicable)
- Personal necessities for confinement

---

## Elective Admission (Planned Procedure)

1. Obtain a **doctor's admitting order and referral**.
2. Prepare your IDs, PhilHealth MDR, and HMO documents.
3. Proceed to **Admissions** for room assignment and billing arrangements.
