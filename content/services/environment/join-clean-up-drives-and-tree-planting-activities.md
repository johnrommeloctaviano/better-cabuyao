# Cabuyao City: Join Clean-Up Drives and Tree Planting Activities

The **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** organizes regular environmental programs open to all Cabuyao City residents — from barangay clean-up drives to tree planting and greening initiatives.

---

## Community Clean-Up Drives

Clean-up drives are organized at both the barangay and city level, with increased frequency before and after the rainy season.

**What happens during clean-ups:**

- Clearing of canals, drainage, and waterways
- Removal of illegal dumpsites and roadside garbage
- Shoreline and lakeside clean-ups (especially in Marinig and lakeshore barangays)
- Post-typhoon debris clearing

**How to join:**

1. **Monitor your Barangay Facebook page and Barangay Hall announcements** — clean-up drives are announced barangay by barangay.
2. **Watch the Cabuyao City LGU Facebook page** for citywide and CENRO-organized events.
3. Show up at the designated time and location. Bring gloves and wear appropriate clothing.
4. Participate in national initiatives like **Brigada Kalikasan** and **World Cleanup Day** when the city joins.

---

## Tree Planting and Urban Greening

### Annual Tree-Growing Activities

The city conducts tree planting programs in coordination with the **Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR)**:

- **Bamboo planting** — along riverbanks and upland areas for erosion control and climate resilience (regional DENR programs have planted thousands of bamboo propagules)
- **Roadside and park greening** — maintaining and expanding green corridors along city roads
- **Reforestation focus areas** — upland barangays like **Casile** for watershed and slope protection

**How to join tree planting events:**

1. Monitor **CENRO** and **LGU Cabuyao** announcements for scheduled planting activities.
2. Register with the CENRO if you want to volunteer as an individual or as an organization.
3. Schools and organizations can request to coordinate a tree planting activity with CENRO.

### Urban Green Spaces

CENRO maintains:

- City parks and public green areas
- Roadside landscaping and greenery
- Riverbank plantings

## Environmental Education Programs

CENRO also conducts **IEC (Information, Education, Communication) campaigns** that residents can participate in:

- Waste segregation seminars (at barangay halls and schools)
- Water conservation and Laguna de Bay protection awareness
- Environmental responsibility campaigns for households and businesses
- Earth Hour participation (energy conservation awareness)
