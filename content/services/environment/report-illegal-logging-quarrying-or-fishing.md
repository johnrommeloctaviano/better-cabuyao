# Cabuyao City: Report Environmental Violations

Environmental violations in Cabuyao City — including illegal dumping, open burning, illegal fishing, and industrial pollution — can be reported to the **CENRO**, your barangay, and national agencies.

---

## What to Report

| Violation Type        | Examples                                                                                   | Where to Report         |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------- |
| Illegal dumping       | Garbage thrown in canals, vacant lots, roadsides, or the lakeshore                         | Barangay Hall → CENRO   |
| Open burning          | Burning garbage, leaves, or plastics in residential or open areas                          | Barangay Hall → CENRO   |
| Illegal fishing       | Illegal fish pens, fishing with prohibited gear, dynamite/cyanide fishing in Laguna de Bay | CENRO → BFAR / LLDA     |
| Water pollution       | Discharge of untreated wastewater into canals or the lake                                  | CENRO → DENR-EMB / LLDA |
| Industrial pollution  | Smoke, chemical discharge, or illegal dumping by factories                                 | CENRO → DENR-EMB        |
| Illegal quarrying     | Unauthorized extraction of sand, gravel, or rock                                           | CENRO → DENR-MGB        |
| Waste non-segregation | Repeated mixing of waste in violation of RA 9003                                           | Barangay → CENRO        |

---

## How to Report

**Step 1: Gather basic information (if safe)**

- Location (barangay, street, or GPS coordinates)
- What you observed (type of violation, brief description)
- Date and time
- Photos or video if safe to take

**Step 2: Report to Your Barangay Hall**

- Inform the **Barangay Captain**, **Barangay Secretary**, or **Barangay Environmental Officer**.
- The barangay can issue initial notices and take action for minor violations.

**Step 3: File a Complaint at CENRO (for serious or unresolved cases)**

- Visit: **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO), Cabuyao City Hall**
- Bring your valid ID and any documentation you have.

**Step 4: Escalate to National Agencies (if needed)**

- **DENR-EMB Region IV-A** — industrial emissions, water quality violations, illegal quarrying
- **Laguna Lake Development Authority (LLDA)** — illegal fish pens, lake pollution, shoreline violations
- **Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR)** — illegal fishing methods

---

## Industrial Environmental Compliance (Cabuyao-Specific)

Because Cabuyao is a major industrial city, CENRO and DENR-EMB actively monitor industrial zones:

- **Factories and manufacturers** are required to secure an Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC), maintain a Pollution Control Officer (PCO), operate wastewater treatment systems, and control air emissions.
- **Industrial barangays** like Pulo, Mamatid, and the Banay-Banay logistics corridor are priority zones for compliance monitoring.

To report suspected industrial violations, contact **CENRO** or file directly with **DENR-EMB Region IV-A**.

---

## Laguna de Bay and Shoreline Protection

Cabuyao borders **Laguna de Bay**, which is under the jurisdiction of the **Laguna Lake Development Authority (LLDA)**. Key concerns:

- Discharge of wastewater or chemicals into the lake or its tributaries
- Illegal fish pens or encroachments on lake easements
- Shoreline dumping

Report these directly to the **LLDA** or through **CENRO** for local coordination.

---

## Community Enforcement

- **Barangay Environmental Officers** can issue notices and summons for minor waste violations.
- Violations of RA 9003 (waste segregation and disposal) are subject to fines under local ordinances.
- Repeat or serious violators may face administrative or criminal penalties under national environmental laws.

---

## Key Contacts

- **City Environment & Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** — Cabuyao City Hall (all local environmental violations)
- **Your Barangay Hall** — initial reporting, minor violations
- **DENR-EMB Region IV-A (CALABARZON)** — industrial emissions, water quality, quarrying
- **Laguna Lake Development Authority (LLDA)** — lake and shoreline violations
- **BFAR Region IV-A** — illegal fishing
